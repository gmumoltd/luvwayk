Bible Club Curriculum

A 10-year, field-tested Bible teaching curriculum developed by Beatrice Atieno Ofwona — designed to disciple children aged 3 to 19 across Kenya in the GROW framework of faith.

About the Curriculum

Built from the Field,
Grounded in the Word

The LuvWay Kenya Bible curriculum is not theory — it was built over a decade of running Saturday Bible Clubs, Vacational Bible Schools and school outreaches across Nairobi, Mombasa, Kiambu and Kilifi counties.

Every lesson, memory verse, activity and song has been tested with real children from diverse backgrounds — urban slums, rural communities, children's homes and public schools. The curriculum is crafted to be practical, engaging, and deeply rooted in Scripture.

It equips volunteer teachers with everything they need to run a structured, impactful Bible Club session — even with minimal resources. This is discipleship made accessible for the grassroots church.

📖

Scripture-centred

The Framework

Built on GROW

Everything LuvWay teaches flows through the GROW framework — four pillars that shape how children understand and live their faith.

G

Go to Church

Children learn that the local church is God's family and that fellowship, worship and accountability are essential to a growing faith. We partner with local churches to keep children connected.

R

Read & Pray

Every club session teaches a Bible passage and a memory verse. Children are sent home with a daily reading challenge and prayer practice — building lifelong habits of devotion to God's Word.

O

Obey God's Word

Faith without action is empty. Our curriculum teaches character formation — honesty, respect, kindness, and integrity. Children apply each week's lesson to their home, school and community lives.

W

Witness to Others

Discipleship multiplies when children share their faith with peers. By age 12, children are actively encouraged to invite friends; by 19, they are equipped and mentored to become club teachers themselves.

Learning Levels

Age-Appropriate Discipleship

The curriculum is divided into three learning levels, each with tailored teaching methods, vocabulary and spiritual depth suited to the child's stage of development.

🌱
Ages 3 – 6 · Little Sprouts

Foundations of Faith

Simple Bible stories, action songs, colouring activities and memory verses. Children learn that God made them, loves them, and has a plan for their life. Each session is short, visual and playful — building a first love for Jesus.

🌿
Ages 7 – 12 · Growing Disciples

Rooted in the Word

Structured Bible lessons, role-plays, group discussion and verse memorisation. Children explore the full narrative of Scripture — Creation, the Fall, Redemption and Restoration — and learn how to apply God's Word in everyday decisions.

🌳
Ages 13 – 19 · Teen Leaders

Equipped to Lead

Deeper theological study, real-talk sessions on peer pressure, identity, relationships and calling. Teens are mentored into Bible Club leadership roles — becoming teachers, worship leaders and evangelists to their peers.

What We Teach

Core Topics &
Teaching Themes

The curriculum covers a broad range of biblical and character-building themes across each year. Below is a sample of the 12 core topic areas woven throughout every level of the program.

Each topic is revisited across all age groups with increasing depth — so a child who joins at age 5 and stays through age 18 will have explored every theme multiple times, growing in understanding and application with each pass.

01

Who Is God?

The nature, character and names of God — Father, Son and Holy Spirit.

02

The Bible

Why we trust God's Word, how to read it, and what it says about us.

03

Salvation & Grace

The Gospel message: sin, the cross, resurrection, and new life in Christ.

04

Prayer & Worship

How to pray, why to worship, and meeting God in daily life.

05

Character & Values

Honesty, kindness, integrity, forgiveness and servant leadership.

06

Family & Community

Honouring parents, serving neighbours, and being part of God's family.

07

Identity in Christ

Who God says I am — loved, chosen, made for purpose, not defined by circumstances.

08

Peer Pressure & Choices

Making wise decisions, resisting temptation, and standing firm in faith.

09

Evangelism & Witness

How to share the Gospel simply, invite a friend, and live as a light.

10

The Church & Body of Christ

What the church is, why it matters, and our role in it as children.

11

Spiritual Disciplines

Fasting, Bible memorisation, journaling and growing in personal faith.

12

Mission & Calling

God's plan for Kenya and the world — and how every child plays a part.

Delivery

How the Curriculum
Comes to Life

The curriculum is delivered through five distinct program formats — each one reaching children in a different context and meeting them where they are.

📅

Saturday Bible Clubs

The primary delivery format. Held weekly at church venues, school grounds and open spaces. Each 2-hour session follows a structured order: worship, teaching, memory verse, activity, feeding and prayer.

🏕️

Vacational Bible School (VBS)

Intensive week-long programs during school holidays. The curriculum is condensed into daily deep-dives — ideal for reaching children who attend public school and are only free during holiday periods.

🏫

School Outreach (PPI)

Adapted curriculum modules delivered inside primary schools during assemblies and allocated Christian Union periods — reaching children who have never attended a Bible club.

🌟

Teens' Week Program

A dedicated annual intensive for teens aged 13–19. Covers identity, relationships, calling and leadership. Older teens are then mentored into teacher-training tracks.

👩‍🏫

Teacher Training Seminars

Volunteer teachers receive structured training in child-appropriate teaching methods, lesson delivery, class management and how to adapt the curriculum for different age groups and settings.

🏠

Children's Homes Outreach

A specially adapted module for children in institutional care — addressing identity, belonging, healing and hope with deep sensitivity and the love of Christ.
