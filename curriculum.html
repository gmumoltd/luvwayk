Gospel Story

Two Cover Editions
Matthew 26 – 28 · Mark 16 · Luke 24 · John 20
The Easter Story
Traces the full Passion Week — from the Last Supper and the Garden of Gethsemane, through the crucifixion, the resurrection and the ascension. Children discover that Jesus died for their sins and rose again, and that the same Holy Spirit who raised Him lives in them. The Pentecost event and the Great Commission tie the story to their own calling as young disciples.
Salvation Resurrection Holy Spirit Calling

The Christmas Story
Luke 1 – 2 · Matthew 1 – 2
The Birth of Christ
Covers the Annunciation to Mary, Joseph's dream, the journey to Bethlehem, the manger scene, the shepherds' visit and the wise men. Children learn that Jesus is Immanuel — God with us — and that His birth was the fulfilment of hundreds of years of prophetic promise. A powerful lesson on humility, obedience and God's perfect timing.
Prophecy Incarnation Obedience

Jehovah Series
Psalm 23 · Genesis 1 · Exodus 3 & 17
8 Names of God
Introduces eight covenant names of God — Jehovah Rohi (Shepherd), Jehovah Jireh (Provider), Jehovah Rapha (Healer), El Shaddai (Almighty) and more. Each name is taught through a Bible story that demonstrates that character of God in action. Children learn to call on the right name of God in different circumstances of life.
God's Character Names of God Trust

26-Part Series
Various Scriptures · Psalm 145 · Isaiah 40
The A–Z Attributes of God
A comprehensive 26-session journey through every letter of the alphabet — each letter revealing a distinct attribute of God (Almighty, Brilliant, Compassionate, Deliverer…). Packed with Bible stories, memory anchors and real-life application. Ideal for a full year of teaching. Children finish the series with a deep, word-based understanding of who God truly is.
Theology Memory Work Year-long

10 Bible Instances
2 Kings 6 · Luke 24 · Numbers 22 · Acts 9
Eyes Wide Open Shut
Explores ten fascinating moments in Scripture when people "should have seen but did not" — Elisha's servant, the disciples on the road to Emmaus, Balaam and the angel, Paul on the road to Damascus and more. Teaches children the difference between physical sight and spiritual perception, and how to develop eyes of faith that see God at work in every situation.
Spiritual Sight Faith Discernment

Prayer & Worship

Mat 6:9-13
Matthew 6:9–13 · Luke 11:1–4
The Lord's Prayer
Breaks the Lord's Prayer into seven clauses and unpacks each one through a separate Bible story. Children learn to pray for the hallowing of God's name, the coming of His kingdom, daily provision, forgiveness of sins, and deliverance from evil — all through the stories of Abraham, Moses, the feeding of the 5,000, Hannah, Daniel and more. Prayer becomes a living conversation, not a memorised recitation.
Prayer Forgiveness Kingdom of God
All Ages

5-Part Series
Various · James 5:16 · Philippians 4:6
5 Lessons on Prayer
A practical five-session mini-course that teaches children how to pray — alone, together, in crisis, in praise and in intercession for others. Uses both modern prayer photos and biblical examples to make prayer feel natural and accessible. Each session ends with a guided group prayer exercise that puts the lesson into practice immediately. Builds confidence and consistency in children's prayer lives.
Intercession Praise Devotion

Character & Values

Matthew 5:3-10
Matthew 5:3–10 · Sermon on the Mount
The Beatitudes
Teaches the eight Beatitudes through vivid Bible story illustrations — connecting each blessing to characters from Scripture who lived it out. Poor in spirit (Moses kneeling), merciful (the Good Samaritan), pure in heart (Joseph refusing Potiphar's wife), peacemakers (David sparing Saul) and more. Children learn that the Beatitudes describe the shape of a Christ-like character and the path to true blessing.
Character Sermon on the Mount Mercy
Ages 7 – 19

1 Cor 13:4-7
1 Corinthians 13:4–7 · John 13:34–35
What Love Is…
Unpacks every clause of Paul's famous love chapter through Bible story vignettes — patience (Joseph and his brothers), kindness (Ruth and Naomi), humility (Jesus washing feet), forgiveness (the Prodigal Son), and others. Goes beyond the wedding reading to show children that this kind of love is something they can practise in their own friendships, families and communities today.
Love Relationships Forgiveness
All Ages

Proverbs 6:16-19
Proverbs 6:16–19
7 Things That God Hates
Tackles the seven abominations listed in Proverbs 6 — pride, lying, murder, scheming, running to evil, false witness and sowing discord — through stark Bible stories of characters who fell into each trap. Rather than a lecture on sin, this lesson builds deep moral reasoning in children: each session asks "why does God hate this?" and "what does this cost us?" Powerful for older children and teens.
Integrity Moral Reasoning Consequences
Ages 10 – 19

Exodus 20
Exodus 20:1–17 · Deuteronomy 5
The Ten Commandments
Teaches each commandment through a matching Bible story — the golden calf (no other gods), David and Bathsheba (do not commit adultery), Cain and Abel (do not murder), Ananias and Sapphira (do not lie), the Sabbath in Exodus 16 and more. Children understand these not as cold rules but as God's loving design for how humans flourish together. The Sabbath commandment receives special focus with the full Exodus 20:8-10 text displayed.
Law of God The Sabbath Obedience

Revelation 2-3
Revelation 2–3 · Acts 2
The Church That Pleases God
Uses Christ's letters to the seven churches in Revelation to teach children what makes a healthy, God-honouring church community. Draws on stories of Mary and Martha (sitting at Jesus' feet), Daniel's three friends (refusing to compromise), the ten virgins (readiness), Peter's healing ministry and Nehemiah's crowd. Children discover their own role in building a church that Jesus would commend.
The Church Faithfulness Readiness

Holy Spirit

Galatians 5:22-23
Galatians 5:22–23 · John 15:1–8
Fruit of the Holy Spirit
Maps each of the nine fruits of the Spirit to a Bible hero who demonstrated it under pressure — love (Good Samaritan), joy (Paul and Silas in prison), peace (Daniel in the lion's den), patience (Job), kindness (Joseph forgiving his brothers), goodness (the boy who gave his lunch), faithfulness (Shadrach, Meshach and Abednego), gentleness and self-control. Children learn that fruit is grown by abiding, not striving.
Holy Spirit Character Abiding
All Ages
13
Galatians 5:19-21
Galatians 5:19–21 · Romans 1
What the Fruit of the Holy Spirit Is Not
The companion lesson to the Fruit series — examining the works of the flesh listed in Galatians 5: sexual immorality, idolatry (golden calf), discord (Absalom), jealousy (Joseph's brothers), drunkenness and more. Each negative example is presented with grace and without shame, making it clear that these are patterns a Spirit-filled life is being freed from. Vital for helping older children understand the contrast between a surrendered and unsurrendered life.
Works of Flesh Transformation Teens

Ephesians 6:10–18
The Full Armor of God
Teaches each piece of spiritual armour — belt of truth, breastplate of righteousness, shoes of the gospel, shield of faith, helmet of salvation and sword of the Spirit — through Bible stories of spiritual battles won. Jesus stilling the storm, David and Goliath, Zacchaeus choosing truth, Matthew leaving his tax table, Jonathan's friendship with David and the story of the widow's offering. Children finish knowing how to dress spiritually every single day.
Spiritual Warfare Protection Daily Discipline
