Who We Are

Rooted in Love,
Growing in Faith

LuvWay Kenya is a children's ministry operating since 2016 across Nairobi, Kiambu, Kilifi and Mombasa counties. Our mission is the spiritual growth of children through intentional evangelism, discipleship and worship.

We run Saturday Bible Clubs, school programs, feeding initiatives, teens' mentorship, and Vacational Bible Schools — partnering with churches, schools and children's homes to raise a generation that knows God.

What We Do

Our Programs

Every program is designed to nourish children spiritually, physically, and emotionally — meeting them where they are.

📖

Saturday Bible Clubs

Weekly Bible clubs in churches, schools and open-field spaces. Children are taught the Word of God, fed a meal, and engaged through curriculum we have developed over 10 years.

🍽️

Feeding Program

Rice, beans, porridge or tea-and-snack are provided at every Bible club meeting. Hunger should never be a barrier to learning about God's love.

🏫

School Ministry (PPI)

Primary school outreach through the PPI program, bringing the Word into classrooms and reaching children who may never step into a church.

🎭

Music, Drama & Talent

Yearly festivals of plays, songs, Christian poems and talent shows that give children a sense of purpose and joy while sharing the Gospel creatively.

🌟

Teens & Youth Mentorship

Dedicated teens' weeks addressing real issues. Older children who come through the program are mentored to become teachers themselves after age 19.

🏕️

Vacational Bible School

Week-long in-depth Bible studies during school holidays — 7+ locations across Kenya — keeping children engaged, learning, and far from idleness.

Our Founder

A Heart for the
Next Generation

"Our vision is for a remnant generation of children who have chosen to GROW in the knowledge of God — to Go to church, Read the Bible and pray, Obey the Word of God, and Witness to other children."

Beatrice Atieno Ofwona founded LuvWay Kenya in 2016 with a burning conviction that the church must urgently address the spiritual vacuum in the lives of children across Kenya. She has personally trained over 350 teachers, pioneered Bible clubs in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kiambu and Kilifi, and continues to expand the vision to all 47 counties.

With a 10-year curriculum developed from hands-on ministry, Beatrice leads a team that feeds, teaches and mentors thousands of children each year — Several Bible clubs at a time.
