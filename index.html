Who We Are

Rooted in Love,
Growing in Faith

LuvWay Kenya is a children's ministry operating since 2016 across Nairobi, Kiambu, Kilifi and Mombasa counties. Our mission is the spiritual growth of children through intentional evangelism, discipleship and worship.

We run Saturday Bible Clubs, school programs, feeding initiatives, teens' mentorship, and Vacational Bible Schools — partnering with churches, schools and children's homes to raise a generation that knows God.

What We Do

Our Programs

Every program is designed to nourish children spiritually, physically, and emotionally — meeting them where they are.

📖

Saturday Bible Clubs

Weekly Bible clubs in churches, schools and open-field spaces. Children are taught the Word of God, fed a meal, and engaged through curriculum we have developed over 10 years.

🍽️

Feeding Program

Rice, beans, porridge or tea-and-snack are provided at every Bible club meeting. Hunger should never be a barrier to learning about God's love.

🏫

School Ministry (PPI)

Primary school outreach through the PPI program, bringing the Word into classrooms and reaching children who may never step into a church.

🎭

Music, Drama & Talent

Yearly festivals of plays, songs, Christian poems and talent shows that give children a sense of purpose and joy while sharing the Gospel creatively.

🌟

Teens & Youth Mentorship

Dedicated teens' weeks addressing real issues. Older children who come through the program are mentored to become teachers themselves after age 19.

🏕️

Vacational Bible School

Week-long in-depth Bible studies during school holidays — 7+ locations across Kenya — keeping children engaged, learning, and far from idleness.

Real Stories

Lives Being Changed

These are profiles of real children and families whose lives have been touched through LuvWay Kenya's Bible Clubs and programs.

Ann Napeyok's Family

Kabuku, Limuru — 4 children in the Bible Club

Ann is a born-again mother who attends a local church at Kabuku-Limuru. All four of her children — from Grade 1 to Grade 6 — are now active beneficiaries of the Kabuku Bible Club. The program has given her children both spiritual grounding and a safe space to grow.

Shalom Baraza, 16

Bible Club Teen Leader — Kabuku

Shalom has grown up through the Bible Club and now leads his age-mates. He is the captain of the Bible Club football team and has become a role model among the teens. He lives with his father, stepmother and five siblings — the program has been a lifeline of discipline and purpose for him.

Hannah Munyua

Single Mother of 5 — Kabuku-Limuru

Hannah is a 38-year-old born-again single mother raising Mary, Faith, Ian, Allan and Christine on her own as a manual farm laborer. LuvWay's Bible Club gives her children spiritual education and feeding she cannot always provide alone — "I am grateful they know God's Word."

Loise Baraza Odongo

Grandmother — Raising 3 Grandchildren

A 45-year-old grandmother, Loise is raising Bravin, Brownie and Brighton after her two sons left them in her care. The Bible Club provides the young children with structure, learning and warm meals — things she struggles to give on her own.

Kelvin Kalonzo, 15

Grade 8 — Kabuku Bible Club

Kelvin is a bright teenager who found belonging and faith through the Bible Club. His journey from a vulnerable background to a young man with purpose and aspiration reflects everything LuvWay Kenya stands for — "Changing Lives, Inspiring Possibilities."

Evaline Kadali's Children

Kabuku-Limuru — 3 children not yet in school

Evaline is a mother of 5. Three of her younger children — Saisi, Emmanuel and Thomas — live in Kabuku-Limuru but had not been enrolled in school. LuvWay's outreach connected them to the Bible Club, providing education, fellowship and hope while Evaline works to secure their future.

Beatrice Atieno Ofwona Founder & Vision Bearer — LuvWay Kenya

Our Founder

A Heart for the
Next Generation

"Our vision is for a remnant generation of children who have chosen to GROW in the knowledge of God — to Go to church, Read the Bible and pray, Obey the Word of God, and Witness to other children."

Beatrice Atieno Ofwona founded LuvWay Kenya in 2016 with a burning conviction that the church must urgently address the spiritual vacuum in the lives of children across Kenya. She has personally trained over 150 teachers, pioneered Bible clubs in Nairobi, Mombasa, Kiambu and Kilifi, and continues to expand the vision to all 47 counties.

With a 10-year curriculum developed from hands-on ministry, Beatrice leads a team that feeds, teaches and mentors thousands of children each year — one Bible club at a time.
